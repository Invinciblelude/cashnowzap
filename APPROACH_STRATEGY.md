# ZAP — Retailer Approach Strategy

## The Golden Rule
You are not cold-calling the CEO. You're finding the person whose job is to reduce payment processing costs, and showing them math they can't ignore.

---

## WHO TO TARGET INSIDE EACH COMPANY

### The Decision Chain (in order of approach)

1. **VP/Director of Payments or Treasury** — This is your #1 target. They own the P&L line item for card processing fees. They know exactly how much they pay Visa/MC. They have the authority to approve a pilot.

2. **VP of Digital / E-Commerce** — Owns the app, the online checkout, and digital innovation. If the payments person is hard to reach, the digital person often has budget for "innovation pilots."

3. **VP of Finance / CFO's Office** — The person who signs off on large cost reduction initiatives. You might not start here, but this is who approves the pilot budget.

4. **Head of Innovation / New Ventures** — Many Fortune 500 companies have small teams that evaluate emerging tech. They're specifically paid to find things like ZAP.

5. **VP of Loyalty / Customer Experience** — The ZAP Loyalty angle (on-chain rewards) gives you a second door. If payments people are hard to reach, loyalty teams are often more accessible and more open to experimentation.

## HOW TO FIND THESE PEOPLE

### LinkedIn (Primary Channel)

1. **Search:** Go to LinkedIn, search "[Company Name]" + "payments" or "treasury" or "digital payments"
2. **Filter:** Title contains VP, Director, Head, Senior Director
3. **Examples of titles you're looking for:**
   - "VP, Payment Strategy" at Target
   - "Director, Treasury Operations" at Costco
   - "Head of Digital Payments" at Home Depot
   - "SVP, Global Payments" at Nike
   - "VP, Payments & Financial Services" at Live Nation

4. **Connect:** Send a connection request with a short note (see templates below)
5. **Follow up:** If they accept but don't respond, wait 3 days, then send the full pitch message

### Industry Conferences (High-Value)

These are where payment decision-makers go. Attend, get a badge, find them at panels or networking events.

- **Money20/20** (Las Vegas, Oct) — The biggest payments conference. Every retailer's payments team attends.
- **Shoptalk** (Las Vegas, Mar) — Retail + commerce focused. Target, Walmart, Costco send teams.
- **NRF Big Show** (NYC, Jan) — National Retail Federation. Every major retailer attends.
- **Payments Forum** (various) — Merchant Advisory Group events specifically for merchant payments teams.
- **TOKEN2049** — Crypto/blockchain focused but attracts enterprise payment innovators.

### Merchant Advisory Group (MAG)

This is a trade association where big retailers collaborate on payment strategy. Walmart, Target, Costco, Home Depot are all members. If you can get a speaking slot or partner listing at a MAG event, you're in front of every target at once.

Website: merchantadvisorygroup.org

### Circle Partner Program

Circle has a partner ecosystem. If ZAP becomes a certified Circle partner, you gain:
- Credibility ("Circle-certified implementation partner")
- Warm introductions to Circle's enterprise clients
- Access to Circle's enterprise sales pipeline
- Technical support for integration

Apply at: circle.com/en/partners

### Warm Introductions

- **Investors/advisors** in fintech or crypto who know payment executives
- **Payment consultants** (McKinsey, Bain, Accenture all have payments practices)
- **Former employees** of target companies (search LinkedIn for "former Target" + "payments")

## COMPANY-BY-COMPANY APPROACH

### COSTCO
**Why they'll listen:** Costco switched their entire credit card program from Amex to Visa in 2016 purely to reduce fees. They operate on 2-3% net margins. They are the most fee-sensitive retailer in America.

**Who to find:**
- VP/SVP of Financial Services or Treasury
- Head of Costco Pay (their mobile payment system)
- VP of Membership & Digital

**Entry angle:** "You switched from Amex to Visa to save on interchange. There's now a way to cut fees by another 80% on top of that."

**Specific leverage:**
- Costco Pay already exists in the app — adding USDC is a feature update
- 130M cardholders = captive audience, no acquisition cost
- Membership model means Costco controls the relationship end-to-end
- Costco's Kirkland brand philosophy (cut costs, pass savings) aligns perfectly

**Best approach:** LinkedIn connection to payments/treasury team + apply to present at a Costco supplier/vendor event.

### TARGET
**Why they'll listen:** Target already operates Target RedCard (5% discount for using their debit card) which proves they can change customer payment behavior with incentives. They have 100M+ Target Circle loyalty members.

**Who to find:**
- VP of Payments or Financial Services
- Head of Target Circle / Loyalty
- VP of Digital & E-Commerce
- Director of Roundel (Target's media/innovation arm)

**Entry angle:** "RedCard proved your customers switch payment methods for a 5% discount. USDC Pay offers the same incentive while saving Target 10x more on processing."

**Specific leverage:**
- Target Circle app has 100M+ users — wallet integration is straightforward
- RedCard already trains customer behavior (switch payment for a reward)
- Target is known for tech innovation (early mobile checkout, Shipt acquisition)
- Same-day delivery (Shipt) already pushes app-based ordering — add USDC pay

**Best approach:** LinkedIn to payments team + Target has an "Accelerators & Startups" program through Target Forward. Apply there.

### HOME DEPOT
**Why they'll listen:** Pro customers (contractors, builders) spend $10K-50K per transaction. The card fee on a $20,000 lumber order is $470. With ZAP it's $100. The per-transaction savings are enormous.

**Who to find:**
- VP of Payment Services
- Head of Pro Xtra (Pro loyalty program)
- VP of Digital Commerce
- SVP of Operations

**Entry angle:** "Your Pro customers spend $20K per transaction and you pay $470 in card fees each time. We can cut that to $100."

**Specific leverage:**
- Pro Xtra loyalty program gives direct access to highest-value customers
- Pro customers are financially sophisticated — contractors understand cost savings
- B2B payments are an even bigger fee problem than B2C
- Home Depot's digital push (app-based ordering, Pro checkout) makes integration natural

**Best approach:** LinkedIn to payments/Pro business team. Home Depot has an innovation team that evaluates fintech solutions.

### NIKE
**Why they'll listen:** Nike's entire growth strategy is DTC (direct-to-consumer). They control Nike.com and the Nike App checkout. They already built .SWOOSH (blockchain/NFT platform). They have blockchain literacy internally.

**Who to find:**
- VP of Global Payments
- Head of Nike Digital / Nike App
- VP of .SWOOSH or Web3 initiatives
- VP of Consumer Direct Acceleration

**Entry angle:** "51% of Gen Z owns crypto. You already built .SWOOSH. Your DTC checkout is fully under your control. Adding USDC Pay is the next move."

**Specific leverage:**
- .SWOOSH proves internal blockchain capability and executive buy-in
- Nike App controls end-to-end checkout — no third-party POS needed
- Gen Z is Nike's core demo and the most crypto-native generation
- Premium pricing means higher average transaction = higher fee savings per order

**Best approach:** LinkedIn to digital/payments team. Reference .SWOOSH in the intro to signal you understand their blockchain work. Nike also has a Nike Valiant Labs innovation arm.

### LIVE NATION / TICKETMASTER
**Why they'll listen:** Ticketmaster already uses blockchain (token-gated presales). They're under FTC scrutiny for excessive fees. Chargebacks are a massive cost center in ticketing. Average ticket prices are high ($100-300+).

**Who to find:**
- VP of Payments at Live Nation / Ticketmaster
- Head of Digital Innovation
- VP of Fan Experience / Consumer Products
- Head of the blockchain/NFT ticketing team that runs token-gated presales

**Entry angle:** "You're already using blockchain for presales. Extending it to payments eliminates chargebacks and cuts your processing fees by 80%."

**Specific leverage:**
- Token-gated presales prove blockchain capability and fan willingness
- High ticket prices = high per-transaction fee savings
- Chargebacks are massive in ticketing (disputed charges, no-shows, scalping disputes)
- FTC fee scrutiny creates urgency to show fee reduction efforts
- Festival/concert demographic skews young and crypto-native

**Best approach:** LinkedIn to payments/digital team. Reference their existing token-gated work. Live Nation has a Media & Sponsorship team that evaluates tech partnerships.

## APPROACH SEQUENCE (TIMELINE)

### Phase 1: Weeks 1-2 — Research & Connect
- Identify 3-5 contacts per target company on LinkedIn
- Send connection requests with the short note
- Apply to Circle Partner Program
- Register for next Money20/20, Shoptalk, or NRF event

### Phase 2: Weeks 3-4 — Outreach
- Follow up on LinkedIn connections
- Send cold emails to contacts you found
- Research Target Forward, Nike Valiant Labs, and other innovation programs
- Apply to any relevant startup/vendor programs

### Phase 3: Weeks 5-8 — Meetings
- Take any meeting you can get — even if it's not the payments VP
- Every meeting is a step closer to the right person
- Present the pitch deck
- Leave them with the one-pager (Executive Summary) and the site (cashnowzap.com)

### Phase 4: Ongoing — Conference Circuit
- Attend industry events
- MAG events are the highest-ROI for meeting retail payment executives
- Even if you can't afford a booth, attend and network
